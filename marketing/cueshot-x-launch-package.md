# CueShot X Launch Package

## Launch Positioning

CueShot is for people building UI with Codex who keep losing flow to manual screenshot gymnastics. The product promise is simple: point at the thing, capture the element, and hand the PNG to Codex without drag-selecting, cropping, or explaining the wrong part of the screen.

Primary audience: Codex, Claude Code, Cursor, and vibe-coding users doing UI iteration.

Pain moment: "I need the agent to see this exact element, not my whole messy desktop."

Proof to lead with:
- Native macOS app.
- Menu-bar activation, floating capture control, explicit Arm state.
- Element, selection, window, area, and screen modes.
- Local PNG history and clipboard/Codex handoff.
- Open-source MIT repo with DMG download.

Primary CTA: Download CueShot for macOS.

## Launch Post

I vibe coded CueShot because I was tired of doing the same tiny ritual over and over:

Cmd-Shift-4, drag around the UI, paste into Codex, realize I captured too much or missed the element, repeat.

CueShot is a tiny macOS app for Codex work.

Click the menu bar icon, choose Element / Area / Window / Screen, arm the next click, and CueShot saves the PNG and hands it to Codex or your clipboard.

It is open source, MIT licensed, and the DMG is live.

Built for the moment when you do not want to describe the bug. You want to point at the thing and keep building.

Repo + download:
https://github.com/TheoPsycheMedia/CueShot
